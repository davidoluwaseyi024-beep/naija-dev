Speaker Biographies

Adaeze Obi

Principal Engineer — Paystack, Lagos

Adaeze Obi is the Principal Engineer at Paystack, where she leads the team responsible for the core payment infrastructure that processes millions of transactions across Africa every single day. She joined Paystack in 2018 as a mid-level engineer and rose to Principal in four years — a journey she describes as "equal parts preparation and opportunity."

Before Paystack, Adaeze worked at Andela and contributed to open source projects in the Node.js ecosystem. She holds a degree in Computer Science from the University of Lagos and is a vocal advocate for women entering the engineering profession. She mentors junior engineers every Saturday through her community, SheCodes Lagos.

Talk Topics

  • High-availability system design for African fintech
  • Scaling APIs to millions of daily requests
  • Career growth strategies for junior and mid-level engineers

Emeka Nwosu

ML Research Lead — Google DeepMind, Abuja / London

Emeka Nwosu grew up in Onitsha, Anambra State, and taught himself to code using borrowed textbooks. He is now a Machine Learning Research Lead at Google DeepMind, working on efficient ML inference — the science of making large models run cheaply on low-power hardware. This research has direct implications for technology adoption across Africa, where computing resources are expensive.

Emeka holds a PhD in Computer Science from the University of Edinburgh and has published research at NeurIPS and ICML, two of the most respected AI conferences in the world. Despite his global profile, he returns to Nigeria every year and mentors students through his nonprofit organisation, CodeNaija.

Talk Topics

  • Edge AI and on-device model inference
  • Responsible ML in low-resource environments
  • How to pursue a research career as an African engineer

Fatima Abdullahi

Founder and CTO — HealthBridge Africa, Kano

Fatima Abdullahi founded HealthBridge Africa in 2021 to use technology to fix gaps in Nigeria's healthcare system. Her platform connects patients in remote northern Nigerian communities with qualified doctors via USSD and low-bandwidth smartphone apps that do not require reliable internet. HealthBridge has served over 200,000 patients across Kano, Sokoto, and Kaduna.

Fatima was named in Forbes Africa's 30 Under 30 in 2023 and is a recipient of the Google for Startups Africa scholarship. She is deeply committed to building technology that works for the parts of Nigeria that are not Lagos — the 80 percent of Nigerians who live away from the coastal megacities.

Talk Topics

  • Building for low-bandwidth and offline-first users
  • USSD as a healthcare and fintech delivery channel
  • Fundraising for impact-driven startups in Africa

Tunde Adesanya

DevOps Architect — Flutterwave, Lagos

Tunde Adesanya is the DevOps Architect at Flutterwave, responsible for the infrastructure that powers one of Africa's largest payment companies. He led Flutterwave's migration from a monolith to a microservices architecture over 18 months, completing the move with zero production outages. He attributes this entirely to meticulous planning and thorough documentation.

Tunde also runs a YouTube channel called "DevOps with Tunde" with over 150,000 subscribers, where he teaches Kubernetes, Terraform, and AWS to a growing Nigerian and African audience. His tutorials are known for being practical and refusing to assume the viewer has a fast internet connection.

Talk Topics

  • Kubernetes for startups on a limited budget
  • CI/CD pipelines using GitHub Actions
  • Zero-downtime deployment strategies in production

Ngozi Eze

Mobile Engineering Manager — Interswitch, Port Harcourt

Ngozi Eze leads mobile engineering at Interswitch, overseeing the Quickteller app used by millions of Nigerians daily for bill payments, transfers, and airtime purchases. She started her career as an Android developer straight out of the University of Port Harcourt and has spent the last eight years focused on mobile performance for low-end devices.

Ngozi writes about mobile engineering at her personal blog, ngozi.dev, and speaks regularly at GDG events across Nigeria. She is particularly focused on the reality that most Nigerian smartphone users are not on flagship devices — and that engineers must build accordingly.

Talk Topics

  • Android performance tuning for budget smartphones
  • React Native vs Flutter in 2026: an honest comparison for Nigerian developers
  • App store optimisation for Nigerian and West African markets

Chidi Okafor

Senior Software Engineer — Andela, Enugu / San Francisco

Chidi Okafor is a Senior Software Engineer at Andela and one of Nigeria's most active open source contributors. He maintains three npm packages with a combined weekly download count of over two million, and speaks regularly at JSConf and Node.js Interactive events internationally.

Born in Enugu, Chidi now splits his time between Nigeria and San Francisco. He believes strongly that contributing to open source is the clearest path for African engineers to build global careers without leaving the continent permanently. He runs a free open source mentorship programme every quarter for Nigerian developers.

Talk Topics

  • How open source contributions can land you a global engineering job
  • Maintaining popular npm packages: lessons from the trenches
  • Building developer communities around open source projects in Africa
