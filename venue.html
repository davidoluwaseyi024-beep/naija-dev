Venue Facilities

Wi-Fi
High-speed conference Wi-Fi is available throughout the building. Network name: NaijaDevs2026. The password will be displayed on screen and printed on your badge lanyard.
Parking
Complimentary parking is available in the Landmark Centre car park for up to 500 vehicles. Additional street parking is available along Water Corporation Drive.
Capacity
Landmark Centre holds over 1,000 people across its main hall, two breakout halls, and the exhibition space. NaijaDevs 2026 is capped at 800 registered attendees to keep the experience high quality.
Accessibility
Landmark Centre is fully wheelchair accessible with ramps, lifts, and accessible restrooms on all floors. Please contact us at hello@naijadevs.ng in advance if you have specific accessibility requirements.
Catering
A full Nigerian buffet lunch is included on both conference days. Vegetarian, vegan, and halal options are available. Please indicate dietary requirements during registration.
Prayer Room
A quiet prayer and reflection room is available on the ground floor throughout both conference days.
First Aid
A qualified first aid team will be on-site at all times. The first aid station is located near the main lobby entrance.

Getting Here

Flying into Lagos

The nearest airport is Murtala Muhammed International Airport (LOS) in Ikeja, approximately 28 kilometres from Landmark Centre. The journey by road takes around 35 to 90 minutes depending on traffic. Plan for longer during morning and evening peak hours.

Airlines serving Lagos include Air Peace, Ibom Air, United Nigeria Airlines, and Dana Air for domestic routes. International carriers include British Airways, Emirates, Air France, and Ethiopian Airlines.

Bolt and Uber are available at the airport. A ride to Landmark Centre costs approximately ₦8,000 to ₦15,000 depending on traffic.

Hotels Nearby

All recommended hotels are within the Victoria Island, Lekki Phase 1, and Ikoyi areas.

Getting Around Lagos

Lagos traffic is well known for being busy. Here are the best ways to get around:

  • Bolt and Uber are the safest and most reliable options for visitors.
  • BRT (Bus Rapid Transit) is affordable and reasonably reliable on the island corridors.
  • Danfo buses are the traditional Lagos option but not recommended for visitors unfamiliar with the routes.
  • Lagos roads are busiest between 7am and 9am and between 5pm and 8pm. Build in extra travel time.
  • Staying on the Island (Victoria Island, Lekki, Ikoyi) removes the need to cross the Third Mainland Bridge during rush hour.
Visa Information for International Attendees

Nigeria has an e-Visa system for most nationalities. Visitors from ECOWAS member states do not need a visa.

Applications can be made at immigration.gov.ng. We can provide an official invitation letter to support your application. Email hello@naijadevs.ng with your full name, passport number, and nationality.

Health and Safety Notes

For visitors travelling to Lagos, standard travel health precautions apply:

  • Yellow Fever vaccination is required for entry into Nigeria if arriving from certain countries. Check with your airline and embassy before travelling.
  • Routine vaccinations including Hepatitis A and B and Typhoid are recommended.
  • Antimalarial medication is advisable for international visitors. Speak to your doctor before travel.
  • Drink bottled or filtered water. The venue will have water stations available throughout both days.
